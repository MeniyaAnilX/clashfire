Perfect Sensitivity Settings for Better Aim

Sensitivity settings are the foundation of good aiming in Free Fire. The recommended settings for mobile players are: General Sensitivity 80, Red Dot Sight 75, 2x Scope 65, 4x Scope 55, AWM Scope 45. These balanced values allow you to control recoil at close range while still being accurate at medium range. For players using 3-finger claw grip, lower general sensitivity to 70 to reduce overaiming. Always test your settings in training mode for 10 minutes after any change before playing ranked. Never copy pro player settings blindly — every hand size and phone screen is different.

SIP Investment — The Most Powerful Wealth Building Tool in India

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is the smartest way for beginners to start investing in mutual funds without market timing risk. With SIP, you invest a fixed amount every month — even as little as ₹500 — into a mutual fund. Over time, this creates a habit of saving and takes advantage of rupee cost averaging, meaning you buy more units when markets are down and fewer when markets are up. For example, investing ₹2,000 per month in a Nifty 50 index fund at 12% annual returns gives you approximately ₹20 lakh in 20 years from just ₹4.8 lakh invested. Start your SIP today on platforms like Zerodha Coin, Groww, or Paytm Money — all offer direct investing with zero commission.

The 50-30-20 Budget Rule — Manage Money Like a Pro

The 50-30-20 rule is a simple and highly effective personal finance framework used by financial advisors worldwide. Divide your monthly income into three parts: 50% goes to Needs (rent, food, bills, EMIs, transportation), 30% goes to Wants (eating out, entertainment, shopping, gaming top-ups), and 20% goes to Savings and Investments (emergency fund, SIP, stock market, fixed deposits). This rule works for any income level — whether you earn ₹10,000 or ₹1 lakh per month. Starting early with even ₹500/month is infinitely better than starting later due to the power of compound interest over time.

Build an Emergency Fund Before Any Investment

An emergency fund is 3 to 6 months of your total monthly expenses kept in a highly liquid and safe account. This is the most critical first step in personal finance — without it, any unexpected event like job loss, medical emergency, or urgent travel will force you to break your investments or take high-interest loans. Keep your emergency fund in a high-interest savings account or liquid mutual fund. Calculate your monthly expenses including rent, food, bills, and EMIs, multiply by 6, and that is your emergency fund target. Only after this fund is complete should you start investing in equity mutual funds or stocks.

Fixed Deposits vs Mutual Funds — Which is Better?

Fixed Deposits (FDs) offer guaranteed returns of 6–7.5% per year with zero market risk — ideal for emergency funds and short-term savings goals under 2 years. Mutual Funds offer market-linked returns averaging 10–15% per year over long periods (5–10 years) but carry market risk — ideal for long-term wealth creation goals like retirement or children's education. The best strategy is to use both: keep 6 months expenses in FD as emergency fund, and invest remaining savings in diversified equity mutual funds via SIP for long-term goals.
